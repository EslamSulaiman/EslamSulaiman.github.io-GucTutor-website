7. Trigonometric Functions & Their Inverse Functions (Continued)

(1) The Inverse Sine Function: $sin^{-1}(x)$ or $arcsin(x)$

  • **Definition**: $sin^{-1}(x)$ is the angle whose sine is $x$. It is an angle in the 1st or 4th quadrant. The function $sin(x)$ is restricted to the domain $[-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}]$ to make it one-to-one and allow for an inverse.
  • **Domain**: $[-1, 1]$
  • **Range**: $[-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}]$
  • **Properties**:
    • $sin^{-1}(sin~x) = x$, for $x \in [-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}]$
    • $sin(sin^{-1}x) = x$, for $x \in [-1, 1]$
  • **General Property**: $sin^{-1}(-x) = -sin^{-1}(x)$
  • **Examples**:
    • $sin^{-1}(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}) = \frac{\pi}{3}$
    • $sin^{-1}(-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}) = sin^{-1}(-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}) = -\frac{\pi}{4}$

(2) The Inverse Cosine Function: $cos^{-1}(x)$ or $arccos(x)$

  • **Definition**: $cos^{-1}(x)$ is the angle whose cosine is $x$. It is an angle in the 1st or 2nd quadrant. The domain of $cos(x)$ is restricted to $[0, \pi]$ to define its inverse.
  • **Domain**: $[-1, 1]$
  • **Range**: $[0, \pi]$
  • **Properties**:
    • $cos^{-1}(cos~x) = x$, for $x \in [0, \pi]$
    • $cos(cos^{-1}x) = x$, for $x \in [-1, 1]$
  • **General Property**: $cos^{-1}(-x) = \pi - cos^{-1}(x)$
  • **Examples**:
    • $cos^{-1}(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}) = cos^{-1}(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}) = \frac{\pi}{4}$
    • $cos^{-1}(-\frac{1}{2}) = \frac{2}{3}\pi$

(3) The Inverse Tangent and Cotangent Functions: $tan^{-1}(x)$, $arctan(x)$, $cot^{-1}(x)$, $arccot(x)$

  • **Inverse Tangent**:
    • **Definition**: $y=tan^{-1}x$ is the number in $(-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2})$ for which $tan~y=x$. The domain of $tan(x)$ is restricted to $(-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2})$ to define its inverse.
    • **Domain**: $(-\infty, \infty)$
    • **Range**: $(-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2})$
    • **Properties**:
      • $tan(tan^{-1}x)=x$, for $x \in (-\infty,\infty)$
      • $tan^{-1}(tan~x)=x$, for $x \in (-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2})$
    • **General Property**: $tan^{-1}(-x)=-tan^{-1}(x)$
    • **Examples**:
      • $tan^{-1}(\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}) = tan^{-1}(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}) = \frac{\pi}{6}$
      • $tan^{-1}(-\sqrt{3}) = -\frac{\pi}{3}$
  • **Inverse Cotangent**:
    • **Definition**: $y=cot^{-1}x$ is the number in $(0, \pi)$ for which $cot~y=x$. The domain of $cot(x)$ is restricted to $(0, \pi)$ to define its inverse.
    • **Domain**: $(-\infty, \infty)$
    • **Range**: $(0, \pi)$
    • **Properties**:
      • $cot(cot^{-1}x)=x$, for $x \in (-\infty,\infty)$
      • $cot^{-1}(cot~x)=x$, for $x \in (0,\pi)$

Graphs of All Inverse Trigonometric Functions

It is important to study the graphs of all inverse trigonometric functions to understand their domains and ranges.

  • **$y=sin^{-1}x$**: Domain: $[-1,1]$, Range: $[-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}]$
  • **$y=cos^{-1}x$**: Domain: $[-1,1]$, Range: $[0,\pi]$
  • **$y=tan^{-1}x$**: Domain: $(-\infty,\infty)$, Range: $(-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2})$
  • **$y=sec^{-1}x$**: Domain: $x\le-1$ or $x\ge1$, Range: $[0,\pi], y\ne\frac{\pi}{2}$
  • **$y=csc^{-1}x$**: Domain: $x\le-1$ or $x\ge1$, Range: $[-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}],y\ne0$
  • **$y=cot^{-1}x$**: Domain: $(-\infty,\infty)$, Range: $(0,\pi)$

Key Identities and Properties of Inverse Trigonometric Functions

  • $sin^{-1}(x) + sin^{-1}(-x) = 0$
  • $cos^{-1}(x) + cos^{-1}(-x) = \pi$
  • $sin^{-1}(x) + cos^{-1}(x) = \frac{\pi}{2}$ (meaning they are complementary angles)
  • $tan^{-1}(x) + tan^{-1}(-x) = 0$
  • This implies that $cos^{-1}(x) = \frac{\pi}{2} - sin^{-1}(x)$.

Example: Finding values of basic trigonometric functions from an inverse sine function

Let $\alpha = sin^{-1}(\frac{2}{3})$. Find the values of all basic trigonometric functions of $\alpha$.

From a right triangle where $\sin\alpha = \frac{2}{3}$ (opposite side = 2, hypotenuse = 3), the adjacent side can be found using the Pythagorean theorem: $\sqrt{3^2 - 2^2} = \sqrt{9 - 4} = \sqrt{5}$.

  • $sin~\alpha = \frac{2}{3}$
  • $cos~\alpha = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{3}$
  • $csc~\alpha = \frac{3}{2}$
  • $sec~\alpha = \frac{3}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{3\sqrt{5}}{5}$
  • $tan~\alpha = \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{2\sqrt{5}}{5}$
  • $cot~\alpha = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}$

8. Hyperbolic Functions (Next Lecture)

The lecture introduces hyperbolic functions as the topic for the next session. These functions are based on the exponential function, similar to how trigonometric functions relate to circles.

The two basic hyperbolic functions are:

  • **Hyperbolic Sine**: $Sinh(x) = \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{2}$
  • **Hyperbolic Cosine**: $Cosh(x) = \frac{e^x + e^{-x}}{2}$

Initial values provided:

  • $Sinh(0) = 0$
  • $Cosh(0) = 1$

Students are encouraged to find the domains and ranges of these functions from their graphs, and to prove basic identities using their definitions.
