Advanced Manufacturing Processes

Advanced manufacturing processes are often used for materials that are difficult to machine by conventional methods, or when very high precision or complex geometries are required. These processes utilize different forms of energy for material removal.

1. Electrical Discharge Machining (EDM)

  • Principle: Material is removed from a workpiece by a series of rapidly recurring current discharges between an electrode (tool) and the workpiece. Both the tool and workpiece must be electrically conductive. The process occurs in a dielectric fluid.
  • Applications: Machining complex shapes in hardened steels, carbides, and other difficult-to-machine conductive materials; making molds, dies, and aerospace components.
  • Types:
    • Ram EDM (Sinker EDM): Uses a shaped electrode to create a cavity.
    • Wire EDM: Uses a continuously fed thin wire as the electrode to cut intricate two-dimensional profiles.

2. Electrochemical Machining (ECM)

  • Principle: Material is removed by anodic dissolution in an electrolyte. The workpiece is the anode, the tool is the cathode, and a DC current is passed through. It is essentially reverse electroplating.
  • Applications: Machining complex shapes in hard and difficult-to-machine metals (e.g., turbine blades, jet engine parts); deburring. Does not produce heat or tool wear.

3. Laser Beam Machining (LBM)

  • Principle: A highly focused beam of light (laser) is used to melt and vaporize material.
  • Applications: Cutting, drilling, and engraving of various materials (metals, ceramics, plastics); precision micro-machining.

4. Electron Beam Machining (EBM)

  • Principle: A high-velocity beam of electrons is used to melt and vaporize material in a vacuum.
  • Applications: Very precise drilling of small holes, cutting of fine slots, micro-machining. Similar to EBW, but focused on material removal.

5. Ultrasonic Machining (USM)

  • Principle: Material is removed by abrasive particles suspended in a slurry, which are vibrated at high frequency by a tool against the workpiece.
  • Applications: Machining brittle materials (e.g., glass, ceramics, carbides), creating complex shapes and holes.

Automation in Manufacturing

Automation is the technology by which a process or procedure is accomplished without human assistance. It involves integrating various technologies to control and operate equipment automatically, improving efficiency, quality, and productivity.

Types of Automation:

  1. Fixed Automation (Hard Automation):
    • Characteristics: Uses specialized equipment dedicated to a single product or a narrow range of products. High initial investment, but low unit cost for very high production quantities. Inflexible to product changes.
    • Applications: Assembly lines for automobiles, chemical processing plants.
  2. Programmable Automation:
    • Characteristics: Equipment can be reprogrammed to accommodate different product configurations. Suitable for batch production with product variety.
    • Applications: CNC machine tools, industrial robots.
  3. Flexible Automation:
    • Characteristics: An extension of programmable automation that allows for rapid and automatic changeovers between different products with minimal downtime. Combines the flexibility of programmable automation with the efficiency of fixed automation.
    • Applications: Flexible Manufacturing Systems (FMS).

Automation Components:

  • Sensors: Devices that measure physical quantities (e.g., temperature, pressure, position) and convert them into electrical signals.
  • Actuators: Devices that convert electrical signals into physical motion (e.g., motors, cylinders).
  • Controllers: Devices (e.g., PLCs, computers) that receive signals from sensors, process them, and send commands to actuators to control the process.

Computer Integrated Manufacturing (CIM)

CIM is a comprehensive integration of all aspects of manufacturing using computer systems. It aims to link different departments and functions (design, planning, production, sales) through shared databases and communication networks to optimize the entire manufacturing enterprise.
